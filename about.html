AMANI FESTIVAL

7th edition 2020

The Amani Festival is an Asbl which aims:

  1. bring together through culture and thus offer a space for celebration, far from the daily problems and the aftermath of war, where the peoples of the Great Lakes region can meet, think and commit to a better common future;
  2. restore the image of Goma, eastern DRC and the Great Lakes region in general and show the world the willingness of young and old in the region to build a better and more secure future. use their talent to get there.

AMANI FESTIVAL HISTORICAL AND VALUES

OUR VALUES

Building together
We believe that a better future depends on all of us and that we must work together to build it.

Entrepreneurship
We believe that dynamism and a spirit of innovation are important paths for the development and prosperity of the region.

Environmental Protection
We believe that collective and individual well-being requires everyone's commitment to cleanliness and the protection of nature.

Selflessness
To fully live the three preceding values, we believe that a sense of sacrifice and volunteering are essential.

HISTORICAL

The idea of ​​organizing a big music and dance festival was initiated by the trainers and the management of the Foyer Culturale de Goma with the aim of promoting peace, culture and peaceful coexistence in the region. Young volunteers, trainers of the Goma cultural center all got together and decided to make culture one of the means of expression, awareness, and promotion of peace. Name given to the Amani Festival which means "Peace"

This idea was born following the success of the organization of the sanaa weekend which is a space for discovery and promotion of young talents (musicians, actors, ...) from Goma and artists trained in the Cultural Center. Bringing back the big names in world music and the artists of the region will bring together thousands of young people far from their daily lives and talk about the issue of peace, of living together. Without any experience or training in the organization of such a big event which brings together more than 30,000 people, the trainers and young people of the Foyer decided to give it a first try and make it a great success. With the help of the Festival promoter and some Belgian friends experienced in the event field, the 1st edition was decided for the end of August 2013. One year of work and research to make this edition a great success. Scheduled for August 30 - 31 and September 1, 2013, the postponement decision was made on August 22 following the security situation in the city of Goma. On Saturday August 31, 2013, these scheduled artists, including Lexxus Legal, organized a solidarity concert in Kinshasa in the hall of the WBI.
